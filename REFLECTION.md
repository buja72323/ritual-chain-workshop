# Reflection

In a bounty system, the rules, deadlines, reward amount, commitment hashes, judging result, and final payment should be public so participants can verify that the process was fair. The actual answers should stay hidden during the submission phase because public answers can be copied or improved by later submitters. After the deadline, answers can be revealed only by participants who prove that their answer and salt match their earlier commitment. The AI should help evaluate revealed submissions against the bounty rubric, compare tradeoffs, and produce a structured review for the bounty owner. A human should still make the final winner decision, because the owner is responsible for judgment calls, edge cases, disputes, and the values behind the rubric. The smart contract should enforce objective rules such as deadlines, commitment verification, reveal eligibility, and reward payment, while humans and AI handle the more subjective parts of evaluation.
